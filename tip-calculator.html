How Much Should You Tip?

Tipping is customary in the United States and many other countries. The standard tip at a restaurant is typically between 15% and 20% of the pre-tax bill. However, tipping expectations vary by the type of service.

Tipping Guidelines by Service

  • Sit-Down Restaurant: 15-20% of the pre-tax bill. For exceptional service, 20-25%.
  • Buffet: 10% of the total bill is standard.
  • Bartender: $1-2 per drink, or 15-20% of the bar tab.
  • Food Delivery: 15-20% of the order total, with a minimum of $3-5.
  • Hair Salon/Barber: 15-20% of the service cost.
  • Taxi/Rideshare: 15-20% of the fare.
  • Hotel Housekeeping: $2-5 per night.
  • Valet: $2-5 when your car is returned.

Tips for Splitting the Bill

When splitting a bill with a group, it is generally best to calculate the tip on the total bill before dividing. This ensures the server receives the full tip amount. If some people ordered significantly more or less, consider splitting based on individual orders instead of evenly.

Pre-Tax vs. Post-Tax Tipping

Traditionally, tips are calculated on the pre-tax amount of the bill. However, many people tip on the post-tax total for simplicity. The difference is usually only a few cents to a dollar, so either approach is acceptable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 15% still an acceptable tip?

While 15% was once considered the standard, many now consider 18-20% the norm for good service at sit-down restaurants. A 15% tip is acceptable for average service, but you may want to tip more for exceptional service.

Should I tip on the total including tax?

Etiquette experts traditionally recommend tipping on the pre-tax subtotal. However, tipping on the post-tax total is common and perfectly fine. The difference is usually very small.

How do I tip for takeout orders?

Tipping for takeout is optional but appreciated. A 10% tip is a nice gesture. During the pandemic, tipping 15-20% for takeout became more common and is still practiced by many.

What if the service was bad?

If service was truly poor, a 10% tip is generally considered the minimum. If there was a serious problem, speak with a manager before reducing or eliminating the tip entirely. Remember that kitchen issues are not the server's fault.

Do I need to tip if there is an automatic gratuity?

Many restaurants add an automatic gratuity (usually 18%) for large parties (6+ people). Check your bill carefully. If gratuity is already included, an additional tip is not expected but can be added for exceptional service.
