How Mortgage Payments Are Calculated

Understanding how your mortgage payment is calculated can help you make better financial decisions. This calculator uses the standard mortgage payment formula to determine your monthly payment amount.

The Mortgage Payment Formula

Monthly payments are calculated using this formula:

M = P[r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n-1]

Where:

  • M = Monthly payment
  • P = Principal (loan amount)

Understanding Amortization

With each payment you make, a portion goes toward interest and a portion goes toward the principal. Early in the loan, most of your payment goes toward interest. As time goes on, more of each payment goes toward reducing the principal balance.

Example Calculation

Let's say you borrow $250,000 at 6.5% interest for 30 years:

  • Monthly payment: $1,580.17
  • Total interest over 30 years: $318,861.18
  • Total amount paid: $568,861.18

This demonstrates why getting a lower interest rate or paying off your loan early can save you thousands of dollars.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in my monthly mortgage payment?

Your monthly mortgage payment typically includes four components known as PITI: Principal (paying down your loan balance), Interest (cost of borrowing), Taxes (property taxes), and Insurance (homeowners insurance). You may also have PMI and HOA fees.

What is PMI and when do I need it?

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) is usually required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. PMI typically costs 0.5% to 1% of the loan amount per year. Once you reach 20% equity in your home, you can request to have PMI removed.

Should I get a 15-year or 30-year mortgage?

A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but you'll pay much less interest overall and build equity faster. A 30-year mortgage has lower monthly payments, giving you more flexibility. For example, on a $280,000 loan at 6.5%, you'd save over $200,000 in interest with a 15-year term.

How much house can I afford?

A common guideline is the 28/36 rule: your monthly mortgage payment (including taxes and insurance) should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income, and your total debt payments should not exceed 36%. However, lenders may approve you for more.

How much should I put down on a house?

While 20% down is ideal (avoids PMI), many loan programs accept less. FHA loans require as little as 3.5% down, and some conventional loans accept 3%. However, a larger down payment means a smaller loan, lower monthly payments, and no PMI.

How accurate is this mortgage calculator?

This calculator provides accurate estimates for principal, interest, taxes, insurance, PMI, and HOA fees. Actual costs may vary based on your specific lender terms, local tax rates, insurance quotes, and closing costs which are not included here.
